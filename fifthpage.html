Staying Safe When Using The Computer

Top Tips To Stay Safe

Don't share personal information.
- If a website or person is asking you for your name, age or phone number tell an adult or your teacher. Make sure you can trust the website you're using, look for a padlock symbol on your web browser or has "https" at the start to make sure your information is safe.
Don't give out your password.
- You shouldn't tell anyone the password to your accounts, except your parents. Make sure you pick a password you're going to remember!
Think before you hit enter.
Don't say or post anything on the internet you wouldn't want your parents, teachers or all your friends to see. Once you upload something to the internet, it can be very hard to get rid of it, so make sure you won't regret it and always stay on the safe side!
Watch out for bad websites.
Some websites will give your computer a virus, which might steal your personal information or damage your computer! You can avoid these my making sure you have an anti-virus and don't go on any bad looking websites, for example to download movies illegaly.
Never hesitate to talk to your parents or teacher.
The internet is a big place with a lot of things on it, some of which can scare us or make us upset. Never be afraid to talk to your parents, teachers or carers about anything that has upset you on the internet.
